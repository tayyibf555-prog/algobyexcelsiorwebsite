Risk Considerations

Honest about what could go wrong.

  • 01

    Past performance

    Backtested and forward-tested results do not guarantee future performance. Market conditions evolve and may differ from the testing period.

  • 02

    Drawdown tolerance

    With 2% equity risk on NQ, consecutive losing trades can produce temporary drawdowns. Conservative caps this at roughly 28% maximum; Moderate can reach approximately 42%.

  • 03

    Execution variance

    Live execution may experience different slippage and fill quality than backtested assumptions ($1.80 commission, 2-point slippage).

  • 04

    System complexity

    The dual-logic architecture has 30+ configurable parameters and requires an initial learning investment. Safe-setup defaults are recommended for new users.

  • 05

    Market dependency

    Optimised for NQ / MNQ futures during the New York session and for COMEX Gold via MT5. Performance on other instruments or sessions may vary.

  • 06

    Configuration selection

    Switching between Conservative and Moderate mid-period can disrupt position-sizing continuity. Commit to one configuration per account.

Plain Language

Honest answers to the obvious questions.

What is a mechanical trading system?
A system that follows a fixed set of rules, the same way every time. Entries, exits, stop losses, and position sizes are all decided by the rules, not by a human reading the chart. Mechanical means it does the same thing on day 1 and day 1,000 — no improvisation, no revenge trades, no over-leveraging after a loss.
What's drawdown, and why does the 6.5% limit matter?
Drawdown is the largest peak-to-trough decline in account equity over a period. A 6.5% max-drawdown limit is the system's hard ceiling: if equity falls 6.5% from a recent high, positions are reduced. The number is published because hidden drawdown limits are a tell.
What broker do I need?
Any broker that accepts TradingView webhook orders. Most retail UK brokers do. The setup happens on the onboarding call so the integration is verified live before anything trades.
What if I lose money?
Trading carries real risk. Past performance does not guarantee future results, and the value of investments can go down as well as up. The system has limits, but limits are not guarantees. Only trade with capital you can afford to lose.
Is this regulated by the FCA?
No. Algo by Excelsior provides software, not regulated investment advice. The footer disclaimer states this explicitly. If you need advice on whether algorithmic trading is suitable for you, speak to an FCA-authorised financial adviser before booking.
